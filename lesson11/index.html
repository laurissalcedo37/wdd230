LATEST FORECAST

Dry northwest flow in place for tonight will bring sunshine and chilly temperatures to the region. Daytime highs will be a little colder than normal with low 40's in the valley, near-freezing temperatures in the mountain valleys. Winds will be light today as well.
On Thursday, patchy fog is possible in valley locations once again, otherwise partly cloudy skies and near seasonal temperatures are expected for Thanksgiving. Temperatures will climb into mid 40's in the valley, mid to upper 30's in the mountain valleys.
Clouds will increase again on Friday as a storm passes north of our area, however, a few rain/snow showers may clip our northern mountain regions. Dry weather will settle in for all areas over the weekend with building high-pressure overhead, valley inversions will likely strengthen as well. Temperatures are forecast to climb back into the 50's on Saturday and Sunday.
By Marcos Guadarrama
